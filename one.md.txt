Step 1: Filing the patent application 
You or your patent agent has:


Filed the patent application.

Paid the required government fees.

Received an Application Number.


This application number is very important. 
Keep it safe because you will use it to track the status.



Step 2: Publication of the application

Normally, 
your patent application is published after 18 months from the filing date or priority date.


However, if you have requested Early Publication and paid the fee, 
the application is usually published within about 1 month.


Once published, the details become visible on the Indian Patent Office website.



Step 3: Request for Examination 

Filing the patent is not enough. 
Someone must file a Request for Examination or Expedited Examination.


Without this request, the Patent Office will not examine your application, 
and it will not proceed toward grant.


Usually, patent agents file this for you, but you should confirm with them.
